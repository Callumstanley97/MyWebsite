1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business :

There are several purposes for technology in modern businesses. One of which is communication with customers, it is necessary for employees to interact with clients quickly and clearly. Websites allow customers to find answers to their questions outside of work hours, which is more efficient. Another purpose is security, most businesses are vulnerable to security threats and vandalism. Technology can be used to protect financial data, confidential executive decisions and other information that leads to competitive advantages. Last but not least, technology helps with efficiency of operations. Modern technology helps a business understand its cash flow and preserve precious resources. Warehouse inventory technologies help companies understand how best to manage the costs of holding products. Also, it helps the employees understand how much stock they have and if they need to order more.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects to fail? Why? :

There are a lot of risks when it comes to being successful in digital projects. Some of the main risks are cost risks, typically escalation of the project costs, schedule risks and performance risks. If a project takes longer than planned it could cause a number of problems such as increasing costs and delay project benefits. If the team you’re working with isn’t putting in 100% effort, then it’s very likely that the project is going to move a lot slower. Another risk projects take could be your company merging with another company which has a very different market strategy. There is also a possibility that other companies may release something very similar to what you’re working on and it may draw customers away from your project. Having too many stakeholders can cause the project to become muddied due to too many committee decisions. A project needs creative direction and whoever influences this should make the direction of the project clear to everyone from the start.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects - How does GDPR impact digital projects? :

GDPR has permanently changed the way a business collects, stores and uses customer data. GDPR stands for “General Data Protection Regulation”. It applies to all companies selling to and storing personal data about customers in Europe. GDPR ensures that information is being securely protected across Europe. Personal data is any information related to a person such as a name or a photo. GDPR has caused the customer to be in control and the task of complying comes down to businesses and organizations. All companies that work with personal data have to appoint a data protection officer who is in charge of GDPR compliance.
